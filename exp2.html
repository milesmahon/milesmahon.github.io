The falling raindrops had a difficult time finding their way to the ground. They were urged forward—or, down, perhaps—by something they couldn't see. He told another:why don't you quit going that way? there's nothing telling you where to go, can't you tell? But the others weren't convinced. I wasn't convinced, it felt like I was falling down. Before long, some of the drops started to get cold. Some couldn't move much anymore—except down, of course. Some couldn't move at all—except down. This made the descent even more difficult. But only for the other drops.looks like they never had a choice. frozen, just falling without any way to stop themselves. if only i could help. if only they could help themselves.But not everyone felt like that. He felt pity for them, like we did, but for a different reason.

On the ground, I felt it start to rain.
